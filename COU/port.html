Optimizing Convenience Store Location in Liverpool Using GIS

  • Objective: The project aims to determine the optimal site for a new convenience store with a click and collect facility for Marks & Spencer in Liverpool using Geographic Information Systems (GIS).
  • Factors Considered: The analysis takes into account various factors such as online grocery shopping prevalence, working population, household size, affluence, and competition from other retailers.
  • Methodology: Internet User Classification (IUC) 2018 data was re-coded into categories based on online grocery shopping prevalence. Additional datasets from Nomis Business Register and Employment Survey 2020, IMD 2019, Census 2011, and Geolytix Open Data were utilized.
  • Competition Analysis: Fixed Distance and Drive Distance catchments were plotted for all convenience stores and supermarkets with click and collect facilities to understand and avoid revenue cannibalization.
  • Optimal Site Identification: Potential locations were identified by intersecting high online grocery shopping LSOAs with chosen retailer's catchments, considering factors like major employers, visibility, and road connectivity.
  • Data Visualization: Various maps were created, including online grocery shopping prevalence, deprived areas, food store catchments, daytime population, and household density, to support site selection.
  • Proposed Site: The optimal site for the new convenience store is near Liverpool Hope University, which has a high working population and no nearby competing stores, providing a significant opportunity.
  • Conclusion: The chosen site offers a strategic advantage due to its location near a large student population and the lack of nearby convenience stores, which can potentially attract a high number of customers.

Geo-Demographic Insights for Bay Area Policy Making

  • Objective: To analyze various datasets related to the Bay Area and extract useful insights for policymakers using Geographic Data Science techniques.
  • Data Sources: Utilized datasets include Airbnb listings, socio-economic metrics from the American Community Survey (ACS), vehicle counts, and electricity utility rates.
  • GIS Methods: Employed Geographic Information Systems (GIS) and R programming to map and analyze data, using EPSG: 2227 - NAD 83 California zone 3 (ftUS) projection for accurate spatial representation.
  • Data Preprocessing: Cleaned and merged datasets, including Airbnb data, ACS data for median household income and internet use, and additional datasets on vehicle counts and electricity rates.
  • Spatial Analysis: Conducted spatial joins to overlay Airbnb listings with ZIP code areas, performed spatial clustering using K-means, and calculated spatial auto-correlation metrics like Moran's I.
  • Visualization: Created various maps to visualize Airbnb listings, median household income, internet usage, and other socio-economic indicators across the Bay Area.
  • Findings: Identified distinct neighborhood typologies such as tourist-focused areas, residential areas, and economically diverse areas based on clustering results and socio-economic indicators.
  • Policy Implications: Provided recommendations for targeting areas in need of development, managing tourist activities, improving public transportation, and enhancing community services based on spatial analysis findings.
